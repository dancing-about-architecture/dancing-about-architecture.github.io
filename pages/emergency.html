Emergency Broadcast Network – Get Down

EBN is a relatively obscure group out of Rhode Island working in the honored (at least by me) tradition of media terrorism. Much, if not all, of their collages are made up of stolen broadcast signals, shattered and spliced and regurgitated at high volume. Not as notorious (or as clever) as Negativland, EBN has, nevertheless, carved out a nice niche for themselves in the ongoing culture war between the media givers and the receivers – even über-cool producer Brian Eno recorded a couple of tracks for them.

Their best and most clever track is Get Down. Starting (and ending) with a really irritating trumpet squeal, Get Down quickly explodes in the kind of layered sonic shrapnel that used to delight Public Enemy’s notorious producers, the Bomb Squad. The joke of the track is in using soundbites of people yelling, “get down” over the explosive dance beats. But instead of invoking the archetypal disco phrase, all these bites are stolen from TV shows and movies in which some crazed gunman is yelling at a bunch of hostages to get down. The schism is delicious. I especially like the one guy, who sounds suspiciously like Michael J. Fox, frantically shouting “I’m getting down! I’m getting down!,” like he was terrified of his own inner funkiness. The subtext is worth savoring, but, unfortunately, no other track on the disk approaches the sublime juxtaposition of funk and fear that they achieved with Get Down.

Emergency Broadcast Network was doomed to suffer the fate that awaits anybody who directly criticizes or confronts the established media conglomerates: total obscurity. I would be surprised if you could find them or this release anywhere these days. Someday, some lone gunman may succeed in bringing down one of these media Goliaths, but in the meantime, there’s a big stack of Davids moldering in the basement.
